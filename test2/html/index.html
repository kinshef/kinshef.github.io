Frequently Asked Questions

Still Wondering About Something?

Check out our quick answers below. And if you still need help, visit our How to Play page.

What is Jackbox Games?

Jackbox Games is a party game studio best known for titles like YOU DON’T KNOW JACK, Quiplash, Fibbage, Drawful, and Trivia Murder Party. Picture us as the spiritual successor to old-school social games — that quirky kid who left college early and won hearts by launching a jam business.

You might’ve heard of us via a stream, ad, social post, or a recommendation from a friend. Either way — welcome! We’re here to make your get-togethers unexpectedly weird, wildly fun, and downright unforgettable.

What’s a Party Pack?

We make collections of games designed to suit any group — whether you're together in person, on a Zoom call, or streaming. From trivia and drawing games to writing challenges and social deception, there’s something for everyone.

Since 2014, we’ve released a new Party Pack each year, with five different games inside. Most support 1-8 players, but if your crew is bigger (1 lucky you, and 2 no worries) — many games let extra people join as audience members and play along.

We’ve also got some stand-alone gems, like Drawful 2, outside the main packs.

Okay, I bought a pack — what do my friends need to join?

Nothing but a phone or web-enabled device! No apps, no purchases. As long as they can access a browser and see your screen, they're in the game.

How do I start playing a Jackbox Game?

After buying a Party Pack and gathering your friends, just launch a game and go. We’ve got handy video tutorials and step-by-step guides to help everyone connect and join.

Just make sure all players (in person or remote) can see and hear your screen — they'll need to view the room code and game prompts to participate.

What if I have issues while playing?

Check out our support page packed with troubleshooting tips. If you still need help, you can submit a ticket to our team — they aim to respond within 1-3 business days (a bit longer during holidays).

Pro tip: if a player's device disconnects mid-game, tell them to refresh their browser. Nine times out of ten, it’ll fix things. If not — hit us up. No party guest gets left behind.
